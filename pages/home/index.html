What got me into UX

Before I start my journey as a designer I used to be on-site support for the medical staffs of the university hospital of Montpellier. Most of our interventions were related to users being confused by questionnable design choices that made the user journey complicated rather than bugs. Consequently I came to realise the impact of design on the tools used by an organisation.

An analogy with scuba-diving

Starting a project is like diving. You do not do that anywhere, anytime and anyhow. You need to know your objective, prepare your journey and get ready to reconsider it at any time once you are under water. You need to ensure people around you are not lost or overwhelemed. It is not just about looking at nice things but also making sure everyone is at the same page and supporting each other.

How I approach approach design problems

Sometimes the complexity of a project is not defined by its size but the problems it aims to solve. When commitments and deadlines get in the way it may be a recipe for disaster. I make sure my approach to design is adapted to every scenario and I am prepared to any eventuality.

Seeking for advices and support with at-risk clients?

Sometimes a project does not go as expected: internal or client-related cross wiring, structural changes in the middle of the project scope, failed product education or implementation...
